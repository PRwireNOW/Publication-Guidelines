Editorial Guidelines

PRWireNOW publishes press releases of interest to various industries, including financial, legal, technology, and general public sectors. Our editorial team reserves the right to refuse the dissemination of any press release for the following reasons:

Poor Headline: If the headline lacks a clear news angle or proper attribution to the source, the press release may be rejected.

Advertisement or SPAM: Press releases that resemble advertisements or SPAM, with phrases like "FREE," "Make Money," "Don't miss this opportunity!", and "SALE!!!!" are not accepted.

Poor Newsworthiness: Press releases must have sufficient news value. If a release lacks relevance or significance, it will not be distributed.

Poor Writing Quality: Press releases that are poorly written, incoherent, or fail to adhere to the standard press release format (e.g., narratives, advertisements, listicles, blogs, op-eds) will not be accepted.

Lack of Credible Contact Information: Press releases must include complete and credible contact information. Releases with missing or untrustworthy contact details will be rejected.

Keyword Spamming: Overuse of keywords or phrases in the headline and body of the press release is prohibited and will result in rejection.

Prohibited Content: The following types of content are not accepted:

  • Personal Opinions: Content aimed at harming or seeking revenge against individuals or groups is not permitted.
  • Blog Posts: General interest articles, "open letters," and similar content lacking news value or proper attribution will be rejected.
  • Defamatory or Harmful Content: Content promoting hatred, bigotry, racism, gratuitous violence, or maliciously affecting a company's stock is prohibited.
  • Sexually Explicit Content: Any content related to sexually explicit material, products, or profane language is not accepted.
  • Comparisons: Unauthorized comparisons or "piggybacking" on other companies, entities, or products are not allowed.
  • Opinions and Advice: Press releases must not contain advice or investment opinions, including promises of profit, revenue, or investment advantages.
  • Medical/Health Content: Unreviewed, unregulated, or unsubstantiated claims related to health supplements, medicines, or treatments, particularly those claiming to prevent or cure COVID-19, are not accepted.

Expected Purpose of Release: The first paragraph of the press release must clearly identify the source and purpose of the announcement.

Get Rich Schemes: Content promoting get-rich-quick schemes, network marketing, or MLM schemes will be rejected.

Piggybacking: Unauthorized use of another entity's name or ticker symbol in a press release is not permitted.

elebrity Mentions: Unauthorized use of a celebrity’s name or likeness is prohibited unless authorized consent and documentation are provided from a verifiable contact, such as a legal representative.

Self-Promotion: Content solely aimed at self-promotion to boost an individual's profile will not be deemed newsworthy.

Unsubstantiated Claims: Press releases making unsubstantiated claims for products or services, especially related to health and medicine, will not be distributed.

Inappropriate Associations: Content that falsely implies official government association will be rejected.

Unapproved Home Testing Kits: Press releases promoting home testing kits that have not received regulatory clearance and approval are not accepted.

Additional Guidelines for Crypto Assets

These guidelines are specifically for crypto assets and are in addition to the general guidelines above:

Listed on CoinMarketCap (CMC): All crypto assets, including NFTs, platforms, wallets, and exchanges, must be listed on CoinMarketCap as a Tracked Listing or ICO.

NFT Projects & Collections: NFTs must be listed as a Tracked Listing or Verified NFT on at least one of the data partners used by CoinMarketCap.

Mining Rigs, Cloud Miners, and Farming: Press releases regarding mining rigs, cloud miners, crypto farms, or farming are not accepted on our network.

Crypto Scam Recovery Services: Press releases related to recovery services, theft asset recovery, or scam recovery are not accepted.

Verified Contact: A verifiable contact person and corresponding contact information must be provided with every press release.

Bitcoin Mixers and Cryptocurrency Tumblers: Press releases regarding Bitcoin mixers, services, or cryptocurrency tumblers are not accepted.

Copyright Infringement: Tokens or NFTs that infringe on existing copyright laws, including those using copyrighted or trademarked names or likenesses, will not be accepted.

Crypto ETFs: Crypto ETFs must have a tracked listing on CoinMarketCap to be accepted.

By adhering to these guidelines, PRWireNOW ensures that all distributed content is credible, relevant, and aligns with industry standards.
